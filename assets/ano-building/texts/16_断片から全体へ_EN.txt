From Fragments to the Whole / Exhibition Caption:


Observing Interstices

The interstice between the wall and the door is attached to the wall. Nevertheless, it takes on the color of the door, and changes its appearance according to the opening and closing of the door.
When the door is closed: the interstice appears as if protruding from the wall, and is separated as a single fragment. (Fragmentation / Separation)
When the door is open: the interstice is assimilated as part of the door, and integrated into the whole. (Wholeness / Integration)

The interstice created between the column and the sash is objectified as if framing the sash. Its form, behaving like part of the column, seems almost too autonomous despite being an interstice. This area that emerges between the sash and the column neither separates the two nor simply connects them, but contains the possibility of a new relationship.

We want to search for interstices that maintain a degree of abstraction, appearing in the in-between state of separation and integration.
